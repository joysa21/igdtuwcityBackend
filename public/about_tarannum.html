Primary goal

The primary goal of Tarannum is to create a vibrant musical community within the university where students can explore and express their passion for music. By bringing together the Indian Music Ensemble (Rooh), the Western A Capella Group (Accordo), and the university band (Avasa), Tarannum aims to provide diverse musical experiences that foster creativity, collaboration, and excellence. The society strives to harmonize the diverse musical talents of its members, encouraging them to grow both individually and collectively, and to share their love for music with the broader university community.

Description

Tarannum is the music society of IGDTUW, serving as a vibrant platform for students passionate about music. It offers abundant opportunities for students to explore and develop their musical talents.
As one of the largest societies in the university, Tarannum is renowned for organizing a wide array of musical events and competitions, both within the university and at inter-collegiate levels. These events encompass solo performances and group renditions, spanning various genres such as classical, semi-classical, and a capella.
The society's commitment to fostering musical excellence is evident in its diverse activities. Tarannum regularly hosts workshops and sessions with professional musicians, providing members with invaluable learning experiences. These interactions not only enhance the technical skills of the participants but also inspire them to push the boundaries of their musical creativity. Whether you are a seasoned performer or a budding musician, Tarannum offers a nurturing environment where your passion for music can flourish.
